## What Is UO Outlands?

Ultima Online: Outlands is a completely free, fan-run private server for Ultima Online — the 1997 MMORPG that invented many of the conventions modern games take for granted. Outlands launched in 2019 and has grown into one of the most populated and respected UO shards in the world, with thousands of active players.

**Play for free at:** [uooutlands.com](https://uooutlands.com)

### Why Outlands Is Special

- **Free to play** — zero cost, no subscriptions, no pay-to-win
- **Custom world** — entirely new map, Sosaria, built from scratch
- **Skill-based** — no classes, no levels. You are what you train.
- **Full loot PvP** — in most of the world, if you die, you lose everything on your body
- **Player-driven economy** — crafters, tamers, fighters, and merchants all need each other
- **Constant development** — the Outlands team updates the game regularly with new content, balance changes, and events
- **Enormous depth** — a template you think is simple will reveal layers you didn't expect

### How It Differs from Classic UO

Outlands isn't a classic replica — it's a reimagining:
- Entirely custom map with unique dungeons, regions, and lore
- Rebalanced skills and systems
- Custom crafting progression
- Dungeon map system (instanced-ish dungeon content)
- Auction house
- Modified PvP rules
- Housing in dedicated housing areas
- Regular seasonal events and content

## Getting Started

### Installation

1. Download the **Outlands installer** from [uooutlands.com](https://uooutlands.com/wiki/Getting_Started)
2. The client installs automatically — no UO licence needed
3. Create a free account on the website
4. Launch, create your character, and log in

### Creating Your Character

Character creation asks for a name and a starting template. **Don't stress the starting template** — you can change any skill after creation. Starting templates just give you a small head start.

**Recommended starting choices:**

| Goal | Starting Template |
|---|---|
| Combat (melee) | Warrior |
| Magic | Mage |
| Ranged combat | Archer |
| Pets and taming | Tamer |
| Making money crafting | Crafter |

### Your First 30 Minutes

1. **Spawn in Britain** — the main starting city
2. **Talk to the New Player Guide NPC** — gives starter equipment and explains basics
3. **Open your Skills window** (`Alt+S` or the skills paperdoll button)
4. **Find a training dummy** in Britain — attack it to train your weapon skill
5. **Read the paper in your backpack** — it has starter quests with good rewards
6. **Set your skill locks** — critical step (explained below)

## The Skill System

This is the core of Ultima Online. There are no character levels. There are no classes. There is only your skill set — a collection of up to 700 total skill points distributed however you choose across dozens of skills.

### Skill Basics

- Each skill ranges from **0.0 to 100.0**
- Your character has a **700 total skill cap** — you cannot exceed this across all skills
- Skills train by **using them** — cast spells to raise Magery, hit things to raise Swordsmanship
- Each skill has a **lock state:**
  - **Up arrow (↑)** — skill will gain when used
  - **Down arrow (↓)** — skill will lose when you exceed the cap
  - **Lock (🔒)** — skill is frozen, won't gain or lose

### Managing the 700 Cap

This is the most important mechanic for new players. When all your skills are locked up but you're at 700, you can't gain anything new. To train a new skill:

1. Find a skill you don't need
2. Set it to **Down (↓)**
3. Use the skill you want to gain (↑)
4. The skill set to drop will decrease as the new one rises

**Pro tip:** Always have a skill set to drop when actively training. Many new players wonder why skills stop growing — it's because they hit 700 and everything is locked.

### Skill Gain Rate

Skills train faster at lower levels and slow down significantly above 80. The last few points to reach 100 can take many hours. This is intentional — a Grandmaster (GM = 100 skill) is genuinely rare and powerful.

**Power Scrolls:** Certain rare scrolls allow skills to go above 100, up to 120. These are valuable, expensive, and drop from champion spawns and high-end content.

## Combat

### Weapon Types & Damage

Each weapon type has unique properties:

| Weapon Type | Skill | Special Trait |
|---|---|---|
| Swords (Longsword, Katana) | Swordsmanship | Versatile, fast |
| Axes | Swordsmanship | +Damage with Lumberjacking |
| Maces (War Hammer, Club) | Mace Fighting | Stamina drain on target |
| Spears/Polearms | Fencing | Long reach, dismounting |
| Daggers/Kryss | Fencing | Fast, poisonable |
| Bows | Archery | Ranged, can't be blocked by Parry |
| Crossbows | Archery | Slower, higher damage |

### Weapon Special Moves

Every weapon has two special moves. These cost mana to use and activate with key binds:

- **Primary special** — activated with `F8` (configurable)
- **Secondary special** — activated with `F9` (configurable)

Examples:
- Longsword: Armour Ignore + Disarm
- War Hammer: Crushing Blow + Concussion Blow
- Kryss: Infectious Strike (applies poison) + Shadow Strike

### The Hit Triangle

Melee combat is influenced by three interacting skills:
- **Weapon Skill** (Swordsmanship, etc.) — determines hit chance
- **Tactics** — bonus damage modifier
- **Anatomy** — bonus damage modifier

All three at 100 = maximum melee output.

### Armour & Resists

Armour provides resistances to damage types:
- **Physical**
- **Fire**
- **Cold**
- **Poison**
- **Energy**

Each creature and spell deals one or more of these types. Match your resistances to what you're fighting.

**Armour Types:**
| Type | Skill to Craft | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Leather | Tailoring | Mages, archers, tamers (no Meditation penalty) |
| Studded Leather | Tailoring | Mid-tier physical resist |
| Bone | Tailoring | Necromancers |
| Ring Mail | Blacksmithy | Budget melee |
| Chain Mail | Blacksmithy | Mid melee |
| Plate Mail | Blacksmithy | Maximum physical resist, heavy |

### Healing in Combat

- **Bandage Healing** — requires Healing + Anatomy skills. Apply bandages to yourself or others. Heals over time. Most reliable method.
- **Spell Healing** — Cure, Heal, Greater Heal spells. Instant but uses mana.
- **Chivalry Healing** — Close Wounds (Chivalry spell). Fast and efficient.
- **Vampiric Embrace** (Necromancy) — Life drain from attacks.

## Magic Systems

### 🔵 Magery

The classic UO magic system. Eight circles of spells, from basic utility to devastating attacks.

**Circle 1–3:** Utility — Night Sight, Cure, Heal, Agility, Strength
**Circle 4–5:** Combat starts — Fireball, Lightning, Mana Drain, Paralyze
**Circle 6–7:** Heavy damage — Energy Bolt, Explosion, Chain Lightning, Meteor Swarm
**Circle 8:** Endgame spells — Earthquake, Energy Vortex, Resurrection, Summon Daemon

**Training Magery:**
- Buy reagents from Mage NPC shops
- Cast spells repeatedly — gain from attempts near your level
- **Meditation** trains your mana regeneration — crucial for casters
- **Evaluate Intelligence** raises spell damage modifier

### ☠️ Necromancy

Dark magic focused on undead, life drain, and transformation.

**Key Spells:**
- **Horrific Beast** — transform into a beast form, boosting stats
- **Lich Form** — transform into a lich, massive mana regen, undead immunities
- **Wraith Form** — mana drain on attacks, reduced physical damage taken
- **Animate Dead** — raise slain creatures as undead servants
- **Vampiric Embrace** — drain life from each hit
- **Poison Strike** — direct damage + poison
- **Pain Spike** — damage that ignores all resists

**Note:** Requires **Spirit Speak** as the supporting skill.

### ⚡ Mysticism

A newer magic system with unique elemental spells and support abilities.

**Key Spells:**
- **Nether Bolt** — fast, reliable damage
- **Healing Stone** — deployable healing object
- **Cleansing Winds** — AOE heal for you and nearby allies
- **Stone Form** — massive armour bonus, reduced movement
- **Bombard** — ranged physical damage
- **Hail Storm** — AOE cold damage
- **Rising Colossus** — summon a powerful elemental construct

**Note:** Requires **Focus** (not Meditation) as the supporting skill.

### ✨ Chivalry

Paladin magic — holy damage, healing, and combat buffs.

**Key Abilities:**
- **Divine Fury** — brief speed and damage boost
- **Close Wounds** — fast efficient self-heal
- **Holy Light** — AOE damage around you
- **Sacred Journey** — teleport to shrine (fast travel)
- **Enemy of One** — pick one creature type, do massive bonus damage to it

### 🌿 Spellweaving

Powerful nature magic that scales with nearby allied weavers.

**Key Spells:**
- **Arcane Empowerment** — massive temporary skill/stat boost
- **Reaper Form** — speed and SDI boost form
- **Wildfire** — AOE fire damage field
**Note:** Requires attending an **Arcane Focus circle** with other weavers for maximum effect.

## Animal Taming

Taming is Outlands' most powerful endgame system and its most demanding to build.

### Taming Basics

To tame a creature:
1. Have Animal Taming at sufficient level (shown in creature tooltip)
2. Target the creature and say "All follow me" or use macro
3. Watch your Taming skill vs creature taming requirement — success isn't guaranteed
4. Once tamed, feed the pet and bond it over time

### Bonding

Pets bond to their owner after 7 days (real time) and being fed daily. **Bonded pets are retrievable after death** — they stay as ghosts and return when you use Animal Lore. Unbonded pets are lost on death.

**Bond your pets before taking them into danger.**

### Pet Control Slots

Every pet uses Control Slots. Your maximum is determined by Animal Taming skill:
- 80 Taming = 4 slots
- 100 Taming = 5 slots
- 120 Taming (Power Scroll) = 6 slots

Higher-tier pets use more slots.

### Tier List of Pets (2026)

**S-Tier:**
- **Greater Dragon** — 4 control slots, enormous damage and HP. The apex predator.
- **Cu Sidhe** — 2 slots, fast, strong, can be ridden. Extremely popular.
- **Hiryu** — 3 slots, high DPS, fast attacks

**A-Tier:**
- **White Wyrm** — 4 slots, magic abilities, high HP
- **Nightmare** — 3 slots, rideable, fire breath, fast

**B-Tier:**
- **Rune Beetle** — 2 slots, applies curse
- **Fire Steed** — 2 slots, rideable, decent combat
- **Ridgeback** — 1 slot, fast mount with combat ability

### Pet Commands

| Command | Action |
|---|---|
| `All follow me` | All pets follow you |
| `All kill` | All pets attack target |
| `All stop` | All pets stop |
| `All guard me` | Pets defend you when attacked |
| `All come` | Pets return to you |
| `All stay` | Pets hold position |

Use macros for these — typing in combat is too slow.

### Veterinary (Vet)

Veterinary heals your pets. Works like Healing + Anatomy but for animals:
- Bandage your pet to heal it
- Requires Animal Lore to know current HP
- High Vet + high Lore = much faster, more efficient heals

## Crafting

### Why Craft?

- **Every piece of equipment in Outlands is player-made** (with some exceptions for loot drops)
- Crafters are the backbone of the economy
- A skilled crafter with exceptional resources can create gear far better than most loot
- Crafted items can have **exceptional quality** — adding bonus stats

### Blacksmithing

**Train by:** Hammering ore at a forge

**Progression:**
1. Mine regular **iron ore** from mountains/caves
2. Smelt ore into **ingots** at a forge
3. Craft from ingots — swords, maces, armour
4. Use **Arms Lore** skill to determine item quality

**Ore Types (ascending value):**
Iron → Dull Copper → Shadow Iron → Copper → Bronze → Gold → Agapite → Verite → Valorite

Higher-tier ore grants elemental resistances to crafted items. Valorite plate is endgame crafted gear.

**Exceptional Items:**
When a crafter succeeds with a skill check above their level, items come out **Exceptional** — a significant bonus to all stats. GM Blacksmiths with Valorite ore create the best non-loot equipment in the game.

### Tailoring

**Train by:** Cutting cloth, making bandages, crafting leather items

**Required materials:** Wool/cotton cloth, leather (from butchering animals)

**Products:** Leather armour, cloth armour, robes, cloaks, bandages

**Progression:**
1. Gather hides by killing animals (deer, bulls, mongbats)
2. Butcher carcasses for hides
3. Use leather at a sewing kit to craft

**Leather Grades:**
Regular → Spined → Horned → Barbed

Higher grades from tougher creatures — Barbed leather from powerful monsters creates excellent mage and archer armour.

### Alchemy

**Train by:** Mixing potions at an alchemy table

**Products:** Healing potions, poison potions, strength potions, explosion potions, refresh potions

**Combat Potions** (essential for PvP players):
- **Explosion Potion** — throw at enemy for significant damage
- **Healing Potion** — instant heal
- **Cure Potion** — instant cure poison
- **Conflagration Potion** — area fire damage

### Carpentry & Tinkering

**Carpentry:** Furniture, wooden shields, staves, bows (with Bowcraft)

**Tinkering:** Traps, tools (shovels, pickaxes, sewing kits), jewelry, clockwork constructs, lockpicks

**Bowcraft:** Requires Carpentry + Bowcraft skill. Creates bows, crossbows, and arrows/bolts.

### Inscription

**Train by:** Scribing spell scrolls

**Benefits:**
- Creates spell scrolls (single-use portable spells)
- Grants **10% bonus to spell damage** when equipped with a spellbook
- Essential for serious mages

## Ways to Make Gold

### 💀 Monster Hunting (PvM)

The bread-and-butter of Outlands income.

**How it works:**
Kill creatures → loot gold and items → sell/use loot

**Beginner Areas:**
- **Britain Graveyard** — undead, easy, good gold per hour
- **Spirituality Dungeon** (near Britain) — starter dungeon
- **Yew Cemetery** — skeletons, zombies
- **Despise Dungeon** (upper levels) — ogres, trolls, ettins

**Mid-tier Areas:**
- **Shame Dungeon** — elementals, good magic loot
- **Covetous Dungeon** — harpies, monsters, nice drops
- **Destard Dungeon** — dragons (with good gear/tamer)
- **Fire Dungeon** — fire creatures, fire-resist required

**High-tier Areas:**
- **Doom Dungeon** — extremely difficult, best loot in game
- **Ter Mur** — gargoyle lands, powerful creatures
- **Twisted Weald** — fairy creatures, unique loot
- **Underworld** — ancient dungeon, very tough

**Income estimate:**
- Beginner areas: 5,000–20,000 gold/hour
- Mid-tier solo warrior: 30,000–80,000 gold/hour
- High-end tamer: 100,000–300,000+ gold/hour

### 🗺️ Treasure Hunting

Treasure maps drop from monsters. Decode them with Cartography, dig them up with a shovel, fight the guardians.

**Map Tiers:**
- Level 1–2: Beginner, low reward
- Level 3–4: Moderate combat, good gold
- Level 5–6: Serious fight, significant loot

**Required Skills:**
- Cartography (to decode maps)
- Shovels (cheap, from tinkers)
- Combat ability to kill guardians

**Income:** 15,000–100,000+ gold per map depending on tier

### 🏗️ Crafting & Selling

Craft gear and sell it to players. The market for:
- **Exceptional Valorite Plate** — extremely valuable
- **Barbed Leather Armour** — mages and archers always need it
- **Potions** — especially explosion and healing pots
- **Arrows/Bolts** — archers burn through thousands
- **Scrolls** — mages buy them constantly

**Pro tip:** Learn what's selling at the vendors and auction house. A GM Blacksmith who mines their own Valorite can make hundreds of thousands of gold per day.

### 🎵 Bard Farming

Provoke powerful monsters onto each other and let them kill each other for free loot.

**Classic method:**
1. Find area with powerful creatures (Destard, Shame)
2. Provoke creature A to attack creature B
3. Watch them fight
4. Loot the loser

**Income:** Excellent for low-combat investment. A skilled bard can farm gold far faster than a warrior with minimal gear.

### 🐉 Tamer Farming

The strongest PvM income method at high levels.

A tamer with a Greater Dragon can solo most content in the game. Let your pet do the killing, stay safe, heal when needed.

**Best tamer farms:**
- **Destard** — dragons everywhere, excellent gold and rare drops
- **Shame Level 5** — elementals, magic loot
- **Doom Dungeon** — with top-tier pets, best loot in game

### 🏪 Player Vending & Trading

Set up a vendor at your house (or pay to use a vendor house), stock it with in-demand goods, and collect passive gold.

**Best vendor items:**
- Crafted gear
- Potions
- Reagents (mages always need reagents)
- Raw materials (ingots, leather, wood)
- Rare drops

### 🎯 Champion Spawns

Champion Spawns are large-scale PvE events where players fight through waves of increasingly powerful monsters, culminating in a champion boss.

**Rewards:**
- Massive gold
- Power Scrolls (allow skills above 100) — worth millions
- Rare loot
- Fame and Karma

**Warning:** Champion spawns are often contested — other players may arrive to steal your kill or engage in PvP.

### Dungeon Maps System

Outlands has a **unique instanced dungeon map system:**

1. Find a **Dungeon Map** (drops from monsters in the matching dungeon)
2. Use the map at the dungeon entrance
3. Enter a private instanced version of that dungeon section
4. Fight through rooms for escalating rewards
5. Loot and exit

This allows solo or small group farming of dungeon content without competing for spawns.

## Housing

Your house in Outlands is your bank, vendor location, crafting station, and base of operations.

### Getting a House

1. Purchase a **House Placement Tool** from a vendor
2. Find a plot in a **housing area** (designated zones on the map)
3. Place your house — you own it as long as you maintain it

### House Sizes (ascending cost)
- Small House (~50,000–100,000 gold)
- Medium House (100,000–300,000 gold)
- Large House (300,000–800,000 gold)
- Tower (750,000–1,500,000 gold)
- Keep (1,500,000+ gold)
- Castle (several million gold)

### House Features
- **Secure containers** — store items safely
- **Lockboxes** — accessible by friends you designate
- **Vendors** — hire NPCs to sell your goods
- **Crafting stations** — place forges, looms, alchemy tables
- **Friends list** — allow others access
- **Sign** — name your property

### House Decay
Inactive houses decay over time. Log in and refresh your house regularly to prevent it collapsing and losing its contents.

## PvP

### Outlands PvP Rules

Outlands operates on a **modified ruleset** compared to classic UO:

- **Most of the world is dangerous** — you can be attacked anywhere outside the guarded towns
- **Full loot** — when you die in the wilderness, attackers can loot everything you're carrying
- **Murder system** — killing innocent players gives you **Murder Counts**. With enough counts, you become a **Red** (criminal) — attacked on sight by guards and other players
- **Blue players** — innocent status
- **Gray players** — briefly flagged for combat (attacked or attacked recently)
- **Red players** — murderers, kill on sight in most areas

### PvP Combat Fundamentals

**Macro Setup is Critical:**
- Last target macros — click once to attack/spell your most recent target
- Next target — cycle through nearby targets
- Arm/disarm — weapon switch for wrestlers
- Potion macros — instant hotkey for potions in belt

**PvP Checklist:**
- ✅ Explosion potions ready
- ✅ Heal/cure potions stocked
- ✅ Bandages in pack (accessible)
- ✅ Spells prepped (if mage)
- ✅ Reactive armour (if mage)
- ✅ Skills locked correctly

**The PvP Meta (2026):**
- **Mage/Dexxer Hybrid** — magic + melee, very flexible
- **Bushido Fencer** — high burst, poison synergy
- **Necromancer** — wraith form life drain, extremely durable
- **Archer** — kiting, hit and run, difficult to catch

### PvP Guilds & Factions

Joining a PvP guild provides:
- Organised PvP events
- Territory control
- Protection from ganking
- Access to skilled PvPers for learning

**Finding a PvP guild:** Ask in Britain bank (the social hub of Outlands), check Discord, or the official forums.

## Essential Shortcuts & Commands

### Combat Controls

| Key / Command | Action |
|---|---|
| `Tab` | Toggle War Mode (attack mode) |
| `F1–F12` | Macro hotkeys (configure in options) |
| `Alt + Click` | Open paper doll of target |
| `Shift + Click` | Open backpack of target (corpse) |
| `Double-click item` | Use / equip item |
| `Ctrl + Click` | Drop item |
| `Ctrl + Shift + Click` | Drop stack amount |

### Character Commands (typed in chat)

| Command | Action |
|---|---|
| `[help` | Opens help menu |
| `[props` | Show item properties (server setting) |
| `[bank` | Opens your bank (in town only) |
| `[stats` | Shows your current stats |
| `[time` | Shows server time |

### Pet Control Commands

| Command | Action |
|---|---|
| `All follow me` | All pets follow |
| `All kill` | Attack current target |
| `All stop` | Stop all actions |
| `All guard me` | Defensive mode |
| `All release` | Release all pets (can't undo!) |
| `All stay` | Hold position |

**Set all pet commands as macros.** Typing in combat loses fights.

### Setting Up Macros

1. Open **Options** (`Alt+O` or Escape → Options)
2. Select **Macros**
3. Create new macro, assign a hotkey
4. Add actions:
   - `Say: All kill`
   - `Use item: bandages`
   - `Cast spell: [Spell Name]`
   - `Arm/Disarm weapon`

**Essential macros to create:**
- All Kill / All Stop / All Follow
- Use Bandages on Self
- Drink Healing Potion
- Drink Cure Potion
- Cast Greater Heal / Heal
- Last Target
- Last Target Attack

## Progression Guide

### Days 1–3: Getting Your Feet Under You

1. ✅ Complete the new player starter quest in Britain
2. ✅ Choose your template and lock skills accordingly
3. ✅ Train your main weapon skill on training dummies to 30+
4. ✅ Buy reagents if playing a mage
5. ✅ Fight in Britain Cemetery — easy undead, good starter income
6. ✅ Deposit gold in the bank (`[bank` command in town)
7. ✅ Save enough gold for basic armour and weapons from player vendors

### Week 1: Building the Foundation

1. ✅ Get your primary skills to 60–70
2. ✅ Farm the Spirituality / Compassion starter dungeons
3. ✅ Accumulate 50,000–100,000 gold
4. ✅ Buy or craft your first real armour set
5. ✅ Join a guild for community support and safety

### Month 1: Starting to Thrive

1. ✅ Primary skills approaching 80–90
2. ✅ Move into mid-tier dungeons (Shame, Covetous)
3. ✅ Begin saving for a house
4. ✅ If crafting — reach Grandmaster (100) in your primary craft
5. ✅ If taming — tame and bond your first strong pet

1. ✅ All main skills at 100 (Grandmaster)
2. ✅ House purchased
3. ✅ Farming high-tier content (Destard, high Shame)

5. ✅ Working toward Power Scrolls to push skills above 100

- GM all skills in your template
- Obtain power scrolls for key skills (120 cap)
- Build or buy exceptional crafted gear
- Farm Doom Dungeon
- Own a keep or castle
- Become a known PvP/PvM force on the shard

## Tips, Tricks & Common Mistakes

### Do These

✅ **Set your skill locks immediately** — understand what's rising and what's falling  
✅ **Bond your pets before combat** — unbonded pets are lost on death  
✅ **Keep potions hotkeyed** — potion speed wins PvP fights  
✅ **Bank regularly** — don't run around with millions in your backpack  
✅ **Use the Outlands wiki** ([uooutlands.com/wiki](https://uooutlands.com/wiki)) — it's comprehensive  
✅ **Join a guild early** — the game opens up dramatically  
✅ **Set up macros before adventuring** — even basic ones save your life  
✅ **Always carry a Recall scroll or Runebook** — instant teleport home when things go wrong  

### Never Do These

❌ **Don't walk through town in War Mode** — you'll accidentally attack guards  
❌ **Don't carry everything you own into a dungeon** — you will die and lose it  
❌ **Don't forget to feed your bonded pets** — daily feeding maintains bond  
❌ **Don't ignore the skill lock system** — your skills will fill in the wrong direction  
❌ **Don't attack Blues unless you want murder counts** — it changes your entire game  
❌ **Don't stand still in PvP** — move constantly  
❌ **Don't log out in the wilderness with valuables** — you remain in the game for a few minutes and can be looted  

### Pro Tips

🔥 **Always carry Invis scrolls** — being invisible buys time to escape  
🔥 **The Britain Bank steps are the social hub** — buy, sell, recruit, gossip  
🔥 **Purple Potions (Explosion) change fights** — always carry 10+  
🔥 **The auction house is your friend** — best place to buy rare items and check prices  
🔥 **Learn what reagents spells need before combat** — running dry mid-fight is fatal  
🔥 **Keep a Rune to Britain marked** — you always need to get home
