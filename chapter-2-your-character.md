### Days 1–3: Getting Your Feet Under You

Your first objective is survival, learning, and establishing a foundation. Don't worry about being optimal — worry about understanding the systems.

**Checklist:**
- Complete the starter quest chain from the New Player NPC in Britain
- Set your skill locks correctly — understand what you want rising and what can fall
- Train your primary weapon or magic skill on training dummies to at least 30
- Visit the bank and deposit anything valuable
- Buy basic armour from player vendors or NPC shops
- Farm Britain Cemetery for starter gold (easy undead, 10–20 minutes from Britain)
- Ask questions — the community at Britain Bank is generally helpful to genuine new players

---

### Week 1: Building the Foundation

You understand the basics. Now build toward real capability.

**Checklist:**
- Primary skills reaching 50–60
- Move from Britain Cemetery to Despise or Spirituality Dungeon
- Accumulate 50,000+ gold (your first significant savings)
- Set up your macro hotkeys — all survival macros should be working
- If crafting: reach GM in one primary craft
- If taming: begin taming training; get Animal Taming to 70+
- Consider joining a guild for community support

---

### Month 1: Starting to Thrive

You're no longer a beginner. You're working toward mastery.

**Checklist:**
- Primary skills at 70–80
- Mid-tier dungeon farming (Shame, Covetous, Deceit)
- 100,000–300,000 gold saved
- Crafted or purchased proper armour set matching your resist needs
- If taming: Animal Taming at 80+; bonded first strong pet
- Working toward a house (start researching housing areas)
- Understanding the Auction House and player vendor economy

---

### Month 2–3: Becoming Dangerous

Your character is approaching its final form. The game fully opens up.

**Checklist:**
- All primary skills at 90–100 (Grandmaster)
- House purchased and set up
- High-tier dungeon farming (Destard, high Shame)
- Significant gold reserve — able to replace gear without panic
- Considering first Power Scrolls for key skills
- PvP capable if that's your path — practicing in Faction Warfare or guild wars
- Working toward a Champion Spawn run for Power Scrolls

---

### Long-Term Goals (3 months+)

These are the benchmarks of a truly established Outlands character:

- All skills in your template at 100 (Grandmaster), working toward 115–120 via Power Scrolls
- House fully furnished and operational with vendors
- Regular Doom Dungeon runs or equivalent high-end farming
- Known reputation on the shard — players recognise your name
- Guild membership with real relationships
- For Tamers: Greater Dragon bonded and properly developed
- For Crafters: Regular Exceptional Valorite or Barbed production
- Financial security: millions in the bank, able to replace any gear loss without flinching

## 📊 The Skill System

<div align="center">
<img src="https://wiki.uooutlands.com/images/e/ef/skillmasterystuff.png" alt="Skill Mastery — the heart of UO Outlands" width="500" />
</div>



This is the entire design philosophy of Ultima Online compressed into one mechanic. There are no character levels. There are no experience bars. There are no classes. There is only your skill set — a collection of skills you've earned by using them, capped at 700 total points.

### Skill Fundamentals

- Each skill ranges from **0.0 to 100.0** (and up to 120 with Power Scrolls)
- Your character has a **700 total skill point cap** — you can never exceed this sum across all skills
- Skills train **by use** — you get better at swordsmanship by fighting with a sword, better at Magery by casting spells
- The game presents no path, no quest to unlock skills, no level requirement — everything is use-based

### The Skill Lock System

Every skill in your skill window has a state indicator:

| State | Symbol | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Raising | ↑ (up arrow) | Skill gains from use |
| Falling | ↓ (down arrow) | Skill decreases (allows other skills to rise) |
| Locked | 🔒 (lock) | Skill is frozen — will not gain or lose |

**Why this matters:** When the total of all your skills reaches 700, nothing set to ↑ can rise further unless something else is set to ↓ and can fall. New players frequently hit 700, lock everything, and then wonder why they can't progress — their skills have nowhere to go.

**The workflow:** When you want to train a new skill, identify one you don't need right now, set it to ↓, and set the target skill to ↑. As the target rises, the other falls. Swap when done.

### Skill Gain Rates

Skill gain is not linear. Rough breakdown:

| Skill Range | Gain Speed | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 0–50 | Fast | Can reach 50 in a few hours |
| 50–70 | Moderate | Training dummies and weak creatures |
| 70–85 | Slow | Requires relevant creatures/activities |
| 85–100 | Very Slow | Requires optimal training targets |
| 100–120 | Requires Power Scroll | Content-locked — not just time |

The last 5 points to Grandmaster (100) can take many hours of active play. This is intentional — a Grandmaster is genuinely rare and commands respect.

### Power Scrolls

Certain rare scrolls allow skills to exceed 100, up to a maximum of 120. These drop from:
- Champion Spawn bosses
- High-difficulty dungeon content
- Rare creature kills

Power Scrolls are among the most valuable items in the game — a 110 Swordsmanship scroll can be worth hundreds of thousands of gold. They are not required to play effectively, but they represent a significant competitive advantage at endgame.

## Stats

Three stats define your character's physical capability. Unlike skills, stats raise slowly and require deliberate effort to push to their caps.

### The Three Stats

**Strength (STR)**
- Determines your **maximum Hit Points**
- Affects **carrying capacity** — how much weight you can carry
- Higher Strength lets you wear heavier armour without a stamina penalty
- Raised by: physical combat skills (Swordsmanship, Mace Fighting, Fencing, Wrestling, Tactics, Anatomy, Healing), heavy crafting (Blacksmithy, Mining, Lumberjacking, Carpentry), and Camping

**Dexterity (DEX)**
- Determines your **maximum Stamina**
- Affects **attack speed** — higher DEX = faster swings
- Affects **bandage healing speed** — caps out at 100 DEX
- Raised by: ranged and agile skills (Archery, Stealing, Hiding, Stealth, Poisoning, Lockpicking), bard skills (Musicianship, Provocation, Discordance, Peacemaking), Tailoring, Tracking, and Camping

**Intelligence (INT)**
- Determines your **maximum Mana**
- Essential for every caster template
- Raised by: all magic skills (Magery, Evaluate Intelligence, Meditation, Resist Spells, Necromancy, Spirit Speak, Chivalry, Arcane, Inscription), Alchemy, Animal Lore, and Camping

### Stat Cap

| Stat | Individual Cap | Total Cap |
|---|---|---|
| Strength | 100 | — |
| Dexterity | 100 | — |
| Intelligence | 100 | — |
| **All combined** | — | **225** |

New characters begin with **160 stat points** distributed at creation. The remaining 65 points are gained through play.

### Raising Stats

Stats raise passively as you use skills linked to them. The process is slow by design — here is how to push them efficiently:

**Fastest method — Camping near a fire:**
Camping raises all three stats (STR, DEX, and INT) simultaneously. To use this:
1. Craft or buy **Kindling** (use a dagger on a tree)
2. Double-click the kindling to start a campfire
3. Stand near the fire for its full **10-minute** duration — you gain stats the whole time
4. Your Camping skill must be set to **UP** (raising) in the skill menu, even if it is already at its cap
5. Repeat. This is the single most time-efficient stat-raising method available to new characters

**Training Hall on Shelter Island (AFK-safe):**
The Shelter Island starter area has a **Training Hall** with Battle Trainers you can attack repeatedly. Hitting them raises combat skills *and* stats simultaneously. This is explicitly listed as an AFK-safe activity — set up a simple attack macro and let it run while you are away.

**The arrow adjustment system:**
Once your total stats approach 225, you can no longer freely gain a stat without losing one elsewhere. The character status window shows **arrows** (↑ ↓) next to each stat:
- Set a stat to **↑** to gain it
- Set a stat to **↓** to allow it to drop as the other rises
- Set a stat to **—** (locked) to prevent it changing at all
- This lets you deliberately shift your stat spread as your build evolves — for example, rebalancing from a STR-heavy new player spread to a INT-heavy caster spread

**General tips:**
- Never lock all three stats at once — you will stop gaining entirely
- Raise the stat your template needs most first; the others can follow
- Food buffs provide temporary stat bonuses but do not raise your permanent cap
- Equipment (rings, bracelets, special armour) adds to your effective stat total without spending cap points — stack these early

<div align="center">
<img src="https://wiki.uooutlands.com/images/f/f1/icon-bard.png" alt="The Bard — controls the battlefield with music" width="120" />
</div>


Bards use music to control and manipulate creatures. They are perhaps the most unique playstyle in UO — you deal no direct damage yourself, but you reshape the battlefield.

**Classic Bard** — Full bard utility suite.

```
Musicianship       100   — Base requirement for all bard skills
Peacemaking        100   — Pacify creatures; they stop attacking
Provocation        100   — Force creatures to fight each other
Discordance        100   — Weaken creature skills/stats by ~30%
Magery              80   — Recall, Gate, utility spells
Evaluate Intel.     80
Meditation         100
Resist Spells       60
                  ────
Total:             720 (trim 20 points as needed)
```

**Peacemaking:** Target a creature and play — if you succeed, it stops all hostility and stands idle. Essential when something aggros you that you can't fight.

**Provocation:** Target creature A, then target creature B, and play — if you succeed, A charges at B and they fight. You loot the loser. This is how bards farm dragon-tier content solo.

**Discordance:** Target a creature and play — if you succeed, its skills and stats drop by up to 30% for the duration. This is immensely powerful in group content. A discorded dragon is significantly easier to kill.

*Play as:* A solo bard can farm content far above their apparent "power level" by never fighting anything directly. Provoke the dragons onto each other, loot the bodies, repeat. A bard with a tamer ally is one of the most formidable PvM pairs in the game.

## 🗡️ Combat

<div align="center">
<img src="https://wiki.uooutlands.com/images/thumb/1/17/dungeonbanner-aegiskeep1.png/400px-dungeonbanner-aegiskeep1.png" alt="Aegis Keep — where warriors are forged" width="600" />
</div>



### How Melee Combat Works

UO combat is turn-based underneath but appears real-time. Each swing has a cooldown (the "swing timer") determined by your weapon's speed and your Dexterity. Higher DEX = shorter swing timer = more swings per second.

**The hit formula involves:**
1. **Weapon Skill** — your chance to land the hit
2. **Tactics** — damage multiplier (higher Tactics = more damage per hit)
3. **Anatomy** — additional damage multiplier (stacks with Tactics)
4. **Opponent's Parrying** — their chance to block the hit

All three offensive skills (Weapon + Tactics + Anatomy) contribute to your total melee effectiveness. Running only one at high value and neglecting the others costs significant DPS.

| Weapon | Skill | Damage Type | Special Trait |
|---|---|---|---|
| Longsword | Swordsmanship | Physical | Versatile, reliable |
| Katana | Swordsmanship | Physical | Fast attack speed |
| Halberd | Swordsmanship | Physical | Dismounting (vs mounted) |
| Executioner's Axe | Swordsmanship | Physical | Lumberjacking damage bonus |
| War Axe | Swordsmanship | Physical | Lumberjacking damage bonus |
| War Hammer | Mace Fighting | Physical | Heavy stamina drain |
| Mace | Mace Fighting | Physical | Stamina drain |
| Maul | Mace Fighting | Physical | Highest stamina drain |
| Kryss | Fencing | Physical | Fastest fencing weapon; poisonable |

### Weapon Special Moves

Every weapon type has two special moves costing mana:
- **Primary Special** — `F8` (default, configurable)
- **Secondary Special** — `F9` (default, configurable)

| Move | Effect |
|---|---|
| Armour Ignore | Hit ignores all armour resists |
| Disarm | Knocks weapon from opponent's hand |
| Crushing Blow | Bonus damage; can stun |
| Concussion Blow | Reduces target Intelligence temporarily |
| Shadow Strike | Hit from hidden; remains hidden after |
| Infectious Strike | Applies poison on hit |
| Whirlwind Attack | Hits all nearby targets |
| Momentum Strike | Chains to additional nearby targets |
| Lightning Strike | Guaranteed hit; significant damage bonus |
| Moving Shot | Allows firing while running (Bushido Archers) |

### Parrying

Parrying allows you to block incoming attacks. Requires a shield (or a weapon in some cases) and the Parrying skill. Higher Parrying = higher block chance. A blocked hit deals no damage.

The combination of high Parrying with a good shield is the primary source of physical damage reduction for warrior templates — more reliable than resistances alone against melee opponents.

### Healing in Combat

**Bandage Healing** (most important):
- Drag a bandage onto yourself or double-click bandages and target yourself
- Requires Healing + Anatomy skills
- Heals over a few seconds (faster with higher Dexterity)
- Stack bandages — one queues when another is in progress
- **Pro tip:** Set a macro to "Use bandages → target self" — this is your most important macro

**Potion Healing:**
- Healing potions provide an instant heal — critical in tight situations
- Greater Healing Potions heal significantly more
- Set to a hotkey — accessing your pack manually in combat is too slow

**Spell Healing:**
- Heal, Greater Heal spells (Magery)
- Close Wounds (Chivalry) — fast and efficient
- Healing Stone (Mysticism) — deployable, heals on use

### Armour & Resistances

Armour provides resistance to five damage types:

| Damage Type | Creatures That Deal It |
|---|---|
| Physical | Most melee creatures |
| Fire | Fire elementals, fire breath creatures, dragons |
| Cold | Ice creatures, undead |
| Poison | Poison elementals, some spiders, specific creatures |
| Energy | Some elementals, energy spells |

Each armour piece shows its resist values. The total from your equipped set is your protection profile. Match your resistances to the content you're farming.

**Armour Types:**

| Armour | Craft Skill | Physical | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Leather | Tailoring | Low | No meditation penalty — mages/archers |
| Studded Leather | Tailoring | Medium | Slightly better than leather |
| Bone | Tailoring | Medium | Necromancer aesthetic, decent resists |
| Ring Mail | Blacksmithy | Medium | Budget melee option |
| Chain Mail | Blacksmithy | High | Good all-rounder |
| Plate Mail | Blacksmithy | Highest | Maximum physical protection; penalises Meditation |

> **Mage Warning:** Metal armour imposes a Meditation skill penalty. Mages and any template relying on mana regeneration should stick to leather armour. The flexibility is worth the reduced protection.

### 🔵 Magery — The Classic System

<div align="center">
<img src="https://wiki.uooutlands.com/images/9/99/magespellbook.gif" alt="The Magery Spellbook" width="320" />
</div>


The backbone of UO magic. Eight circles of spells, from basic utility to endgame devastation. Magery is the most versatile magic system — it provides transport (Recall, Gate), healing (Heal, Greater Heal, Cure), combat damage, and utility.

**Supporting skills:** Evaluate Intelligence (spell damage), Meditation (mana regen), Inscription (10% SDI bonus from spellbook)

**Reagent Requirements:** Every Magery spell requires specific reagents consumed on casting. Carrying enough reagents is essential.

| Reagent | Common Uses |
|---|---|
| Black Pearl | Most offensive spells |
| Mandrake Root | Most offensive spells |
| Nightshade | Poison, some offensive spells |
| Garlic | Protection spells, some buffs |
| Ginseng | Healing spells |
| Spider Silk | Webs, some spells |
| Blood Moss | Movement spells, Recall |
| Sulfurous Ash | Fireball, offensive spells |
| Fertile Dirt | Rare; high-end spells |

**Circle Breakdown:**

| Circle | Key Spells |
|---|---|
| 1 | Clumsy, Feeblemind, Weaken, Night Sight, Magic Arrow |
| 2 | Agility, Cunning, Strength, Harm, Cure |
| 3 | Bless, Fireball, Magic Lock, Poison, Telekinesis, Teleport |
| 4 | Arch Cure, Arch Protection, Curse, Fire Field, Mana Drain, Paralyze |
| 5 | Blade Spirits, Dispel Field, Mind Blast, Paralyze Field, Summon Creature |
| 6 | Dispel, Energy Bolt, Explosion, Invisibility, Mark, Mass Curse |
| 7 | Chain Lightning, Energy Field, Flamestrike, Gate Travel, Mana Vampire, Mass Dispel, Meteor Swarm |
| 8 | Earthquake, Energy Vortex, Resurrection, Summon Air/Earth/Fire/Water Elemental, Summon Daemon |

**Essential Combat Spells:**
- **Energy Bolt** — fast, reliable damage
- **Explosion** — highest single-hit damage; brief delay
- **Chain Lightning** — AOE lightning; excellent in groups
- **Earthquake** — AOE physical damage around caster; knocks targets prone
- **Energy Vortex** — summons a hostile energy creature that pursues and attacks nearby targets
- **Flamestrike** — direct fire damage; strong against cold-vulnerable creatures

### ☠️ Necromancy — The Dark Art

<div align="center">
<img src="https://wiki.uooutlands.com/images/3/3a/bookofnecromancy.jpg" alt="The Book of Necromancy" width="360" />
</div>


Necromancy is UO's dark magic system. It specialises in life manipulation, undead control, and transformations that shift your play into entirely different modes.

**Supporting skill:** Spirit Speak (required; allows communication with spirits and boosts Necromancy)

**No reagents required** — Necromancy spells use a different resource model.

**Transformation Spells (the core of Necromancy):**

| Form | Benefits | Drawbacks |
|---|---|---|
| **Horrific Beast Form** | STR/DEX boost; claws that deal physical and poison; fast | Can't cast spells |
| **Lich Form** | Massive mana regeneration; undead traits (poison immune); enhanced magic | Takes holy damage; can't be healed by Chivalry |
| **Wraith Form** | Mana drain on melee hits; 25% physical resist; can move through objects | Can't be healed by non-Necromancy sources |

**Key Offensive Spells:**

| Spell | Effect |
|---|---|
| Pain Spike | Direct damage ignoring all resistances — the ultimate penetration spell |
| Corpse Skin | Removes target's fire and poison resistance — debuff that enables massive follow-up |
| Poison Strike | Significant damage plus guaranteed poison application |
| Strangle | Progressive damage over time that increases each tick |
| Blood Oath | Mirror damage back at attacker; target takes damage every time they hit something |
| Animate Dead | Raise a nearby corpse as an undead servant under your control |
| Summon Familiar | Summon a bound creature (cat, dark wolf, shadow wyrm...) |

**Vampiric Embrace:** The passive that makes Necromancy warriors terrifying. Each melee hit restores a portion of your HP. Combined with a fast weapon and high Tactics, a Wraith Form Necromancer heals faster from combat than most targets can damage them.

### ✨ Chivalry — The Paladin Art

<div align="center">
<img src="https://wiki.uooutlands.com/images/f/fe/bookofchivalry.jpg" alt="The Book of Chivalry" width="360" />
</div>


Holy magic. Chivalry provides combat enhancement, self-healing, and some offensive capability against evil creatures.

**Supporting skill:** None — Chivalry stands alone. However, it synergises powerfully with Swordsmanship and Healing.

**No reagents required** — Chivalry spells consume Mana and Tithing Points (holy energy stored by performing virtuous acts or donating gold at shrines).

**Key Abilities:**

| Ability | Effect |
|---|---|
| Divine Fury | Brief DEX and attack speed boost; sets armour rating to zero temporarily |
| Close Wounds | Efficient instant self-heal; best standalone healing in Chivalry |
| Holy Light | AOE holy damage centred on caster; hits everything around you |
| Sacred Journey | Teleport to a shrine; emergency transport or tactical repositioning |
| Enemy of One | Designate one creature type; deal massive bonus damage to it for a duration |
| Consecrate Weapon | Temporarily convert weapon damage to the type target is most vulnerable to |
| Remove Curse | Dispel curses from yourself |
| Cleanse by Fire | Cure poison from yourself |
| Dispel Evil | Push back evil creatures |
| Exorcism | Destroy summoned creatures |

*Enemy of One is the most powerful single ability in Chivalry for PvE — designate "Dragon" before entering Destard and your damage against dragons becomes extraordinary.*

### 🔮 Arcane — The Outlands-Exclusive Art

**Arcane** is unique to UO Outlands and does not exist in retail UO. It transforms the Arcane Staff into a hybrid melee/magic weapon system.

**How it works:**
- You wield an **Arcane Staff** and build up **Arcane Buildup** by landing hits on enemies
- Buildup grants stacking **spell damage** and **damage resistance** bonuses
- At high buildup you unlock **Stances** and powerful **Finishers** via your Arcane Codex

**Requirements to use effectively:**
- 80+ Arcane, 80+ Magery, 80+ Wrestling (all three)
- Cannot use pets or bard skills (resets buildup)
- Must stand still 0.5s before firing spells

**Template:** Arcane (100) + Magery (100) + Eval Int (100) + Meditation (100) + Wrestling (80) + Resist Spells (80) + Anatomy (80) = **640 skill points**

**Why play Arcane?** It rewards precise, stationary combat with extreme burst damage — the highest single-target spell damage ceiling in Outlands at the cost of mobility and flexibility.
